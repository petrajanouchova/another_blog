People of various backgrounds speak to us through the medium of stone, giving voice to their fears, beliefs, and desires, not dissimilar to our feelings today. When studied in large quantities, inscriptions serve as a proxy for the past human behaviour, cultural signalling and as indirect traits of the contemporary social organisation and hierarchy. {% include gallery caption="This is a sample gallery to go along with this case study." %} Although the epigraphic corpora are published as digital open-source datasets every year, inviting for their quantitative analysis, the scholarship specialises either spatially or chronologically. The overarching study crossing the boundary of time and space has not been attempted, mostly due to the technical difficulties arising from combining multiple digital resources, the size of datasets and the lack of methodological approaches dealing with the specificity of epigraphic data.
